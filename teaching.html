Teaching Philosophy

Education • Mentorship • Student Success • Innovation

I believe in creating an engaging and inclusive learning environment that bridges theoretical knowledge with practical applications. My teaching approach emphasizes hands-on learning, critical thinking, and real-world problem-solving in the field of civil engineering and materials science.

Educational Approach

My teaching philosophy centers on three core principles:

  • Active Learning: Engaging students through hands-on experiments, case studies, and real-world applications
  • Research Integration: Connecting classroom learning with cutting-edge research in concrete science and materials engineering
  • Student-Centered: Adapting teaching methods to meet diverse learning styles and fostering inclusive participation

Current Courses

Materials Science & Engineering
ENGR 201
Introduction to materials science principles, focusing on structure-property relationships in engineering materials including metals, ceramics, polymers, and composites.
Topics: Crystal structures, mechanical properties, phase diagrams, materials selection
Concrete Technology
ENGR 301
Comprehensive study of concrete materials, mix design, properties, testing methods, and applications in civil engineering projects.
Topics: Cement chemistry, aggregate properties, mix design, durability, testing methods
Structural Analysis
ENGR 401
Analysis of statically determinate and indeterminate structures using classical and modern methods including matrix analysis and computer applications.
Topics: Force method, displacement method, influence lines, matrix analysis

Student Mentorship & Research

I actively mentor undergraduate and graduate students in research projects related to sustainable concrete materials and coastal infrastructure resilience. Students work on projects involving:

  • Nanomaterial-enhanced concrete durability
  • Supplementary cementitious materials characterization
  • Coastal infrastructure resilience assessment
  • Materials testing and characterization protocols
