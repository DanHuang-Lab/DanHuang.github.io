Educational Approach

My teaching philosophy is guided by three core principles:

  • Active Learning: I engage students through hands-on projects, case studies, and real-world applications, emphasizing problem-solving and design thinking.
  • Research Integration: I bridge classroom learning with my research in sustainable and durable concrete materials, helping students understand how emerging technologies and materials science shape engineering practice.
  • Student-Centered Learning: I adapt teaching strategies to support diverse learning styles, encourage collaboration, and foster an inclusive learning environment.

Courses Taught

Engineering Problem Solving
ENGR 201
Introduction to engineering design and problem-solving using LEGO-based modeling and computer programming.
Topics: Algorithmic thinking, physical modeling, coding logic, teamwork.
Sustainable Concrete
ENGR 225
Study of sustainable materials and construction practices, focusing on environmental impacts and material innovations in concrete technology.
Topics: Supplementary cementitious materials, recycled aggregates, life-cycle assessment, and durability testing.
Statics
ENGR 234
Fundamentals of equilibrium, force systems, and internal forces in structural and mechanical components.
Topics: Free-body diagrams, trusses, frames, shear and bending analysis.
Dynamics
ENGR 244
Introduction to motion, energy, and momentum analysis of particles and rigid bodies.
Topics: Kinematics, kinetics, work-energy methods, impulse-momentum principles.
Structural Analysis and Design
ENGR 365
Comprehensive study of structural behavior and design of steel and reinforced concrete elements.
Topics: Load analysis, indeterminate structures, design principles, and computer-aided analysis.
